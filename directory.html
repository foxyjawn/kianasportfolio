Exit Music
Analysis by Kiana Moore

I wrote this essay for my Introduction to Literature class at La Salle University that I took through a dual enrollment program during my junior year of high school. In it, I describe how Chris Drangle’s short story “Exit Music” both follows and breaks the sequence Freytag’s Pyramid establishes with its simultaneous plots. I have always loved literature, so not only was this a good read, but it made me take time to think about the many ways stories are nontraditionally told. This essay demonstrates that deviating from tradition is not always the “wrong” way, and in writing, programming, and life, I needed to acknowledge that. Additionally, I realized that opposites exist to balance and complement each other; innovation and tradition combine in this story to do just that.

Analysis by Kiana Moore

This essay analyzed the first two chapters of Genesis in the Bible for my Introduction to Social and Political Philosophy course. In class, I learned to interpret the text in a new way that I had never considered before. Essay 2

Plato's Republic
Analysis by Kiana Moore

This essay analyzed Polemarchus’s definition of Justice described in Book 1 of Plato’s Republic. I wrote this essay as my second submission for my Introduction to Social and Political Philosophy course. The original paper was my attempt at a new approach to analytical philosophical essays, but I realized I did not structure or detail my analysis enough to present my takeaway impactfully. From my study of the text, it is clear that Socrates concludes that justice is defined by context. From his conversations with Cephalus, Polemarchus, and Thrasymachus, he extracts a double-pronged principle that neither contradicts itself nor proves constant. This resultant boundless idea demonstrates the complexity of philosophy. Sometimes, there are many answers and, therefore, no single answer, which I felt most accurately speaks to the ambiguity of life.
